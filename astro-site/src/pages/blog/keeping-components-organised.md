For my first few months everything lived in one biscuit tin. Finding a specific resistor meant tipping the whole thing out, and I bought duplicates of parts I already owned twice. A €15 drawer unit fixed it.

## The system

Nothing clever — just consistent:

- **One category per drawer**: resistors, capacitors, LEDs, headers, modules.
- **Masking tape + a marker** on the front of each drawer. Cheap, and easy to relabel when things move.
- **Resistors sorted by value** in little zip bags inside the drawer, because "all resistors in one pile" is the same as no system at all.

## The bit that actually helped

I keep a running note on my phone of what I have and what I'm low on. Before I order anything I check the note. That one habit has stopped the duplicate-buying completely.

<div class="pull-note">// <b>tip</b> — keep the parts you reach for most (jumper wires, common resistors, the Arduino itself) in the top drawers. Future-you, mid-project, will be grateful.</div>

## Why bother as a beginner?

Because friction kills momentum. When starting a project means twenty minutes of hunting for parts, you start fewer projects. A tidy drawer unit isn't about being neat — it's about getting to the fun part faster.
