Soldering looked terrifying on YouTube and then turned out to be... mostly fine, once I stopped doing the three things wrong.

## Mistake one: too little heat

My first joints were dull, blobby and lifted off when I tugged the wire — classic **cold joints**. The fix wasn't more solder, it was more heat: let the iron warm both the pad *and* the leg for a second before feeding solder in.

## Mistake two: feeding solder onto the iron

I kept melting solder onto the tip and dabbing it on, which makes a weak ball. You're supposed to heat the *joint* and touch the solder to the joint, not the iron. Obvious in hindsight.

## Mistake three: no third hand

Holding the board, the iron and the solder with two hands is a recipe for a singed thumb (ask me how I know). A €6 "helping hands" clamp made everything ten times easier.

<div class="pull-note">// <b>safety</b> — solder fumes are nasty. Crack a window and don't lean directly over it. And the iron is 350 °C; treat the hot end like it's always hot, because it is.</div>

## When it clicked

The first joint that came out shiny, cone-shaped and solid felt genuinely great. Now I almost look forward to it. Almost.
