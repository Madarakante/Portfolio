Short answer: yes, for a complete beginner. Longer answer: you'll use about half of it, and that's fine.

## What I actually used

The Uno itself, the breadboard, jumper wires, LEDs, resistors, push buttons and the little servo got constant use. These are the parts you build your first dozen projects from, and having them in one box meant I never stalled waiting on an order.

## What stayed in the box

The LCD, the stepper motor and the IR remote were fun once and then sat unused for months. Not wasted exactly — they got me to *try* things — but I wouldn't have bought them separately yet.

## What I'd add

Two things the kit skimped on: **more jumper wires** (you always want more), and a **cheap multimeter**, which most kits don't include and which you'll want by week two.

<div class="pull-note">// <b>verdict</b> — worth it if you're starting from zero and want momentum. If you already have a board and a breadboard, buy components à la carte instead.</div>

## The real value

It isn't the parts, it's the *permission*. Having a box of bits with no fixed purpose is what got me experimenting instead of being scared of breaking something expensive.
